How to Optimize Images for Telegram Stores

Telegram loads images differently than a standard web browser. If your product photos are too large, customers on slow mobile connections might scroll past before seeing what you're selling. Here is the definitive guide to image optimization for StreamFlow bots.

1. Stick to 1:1 or 4:5 Ratios

Telegram heavily compresses and crops images in the chat feed to maximize vertical space. While landscape images work fine on desktop, the vast majority of your buyers are on mobile. Square (1:1) or slightly vertical (4:5) images take up the most screen real estate without being aggressively cropped by the Telegram client.

2. Keep Sizes Under 300KB

Network speeds fluctuate. A 2MB photograph looks stunning, but your customer might abandon the queue if it takes 5 seconds to load. Use tools like TinyPNG or Squash to compress your JPEGs down to 150-300KB. StreamFlow automatically handles basic compression, but pre-compressing guarantees the sharpest results.

Pro Tip: Text in Images

Avoid putting critical text (like prices or sizes) in the image itself. If Telegram compresses the preview, the text becomes unreadable. Put all essential details in the post caption via the StreamFlow product description field.

3. High Contrast Backgrounds

Because Telegram has both Light and Dark mode, your products need to pop regardless of the user's theme. A clean, slightly off-white (#F5F5F5) or completely transparent background ensures your product is the absolute focal point.
